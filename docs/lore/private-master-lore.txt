Punch Skater — Private Master Lore

Airaway hangs above Greater Western Sydney like a polished lie: cold Blue Mountains air, chrome towers, biometric gates, and UCA executives living in suspended privilege while the basin below drowns in smog and industrial decay.
Below it sprawls the real city: Batteryville steel heat, Nightshade tunnel raves, Grid surveillance corridors, Glass City’s empty neon mirrors, and long Nullarbor-like freight arteries called The Roads.

In this world, networks are poison. Data gets hacked, altered, intercepted. So trust moved back into flesh and motion: hand-carried drives, live couriers, real risk. That is why the Skater Courier became the most valuable labor class in the city—and why everyone hunts them.

Australia’s version of the culture hardened around jousting, not gunplay. Firearms exist but are rare and extreme-use. Standard conflict is Esk8 velocity, cyber lances, shields, and public humiliation at speed. Combat, spectacle, and social ranking are all one thing.

The Core Rule Set
- Esk8 or die.

- Data on a thumb drive beats data on a server.
- A Punch Skater owes nothing… until they owe everything.

Power Structure

The United Corporate Alliance (UCA) controls Airaway and sells “compliance” as civilization.
But the street ecosystem resists in fragments:

- Qu111s (truth-release guerrilla journalists)
- Ne0n Legion (transactional thieves/mercs)
- Iron Curtains (public revolution, internally manipulated myth)
- D4rk $pider (blackmail survivalists with unclear backers)
- Asclepians (medical-humanitarian couriers, also elite logistics)
- Mesopotamian Society (artifact couriers between academia, collectors, cult pressure)
- Knights Technarchy (Dark Lights zealots moving sacred cargo)
- Hermes’ Squirmies (neutral risk-priced labor)
- UCPS Workers (sanctioned postal cover identities)
- Moonrisers (agitator-ravers, spark not engine)
- Wooders (off-grid wood-deck commune)
- Punch Skaters (lowest rung, everywhere, never fully erased)
- The Team (ex-athlete precision operators with sponsor camouflage)

Hidden engine: the UCA/“rebellion” boundary is dirtier than public lore admits; false-flag dynamics around Iron Curtains are part of deeper control architecture.

Geographic Truth

- Airaway: corporate sky fortress; skaters outlawed.
- Batteryville: industrial engine room; Skids’ home; stamina-over-style culture.
- The Grid: hyper-surveilled data district; Skids’ parents vanished into its systems.
- Nightshade: underground origin point of courier culture; loyalty, raves, ambush politics.
- Glass City: sensor-saturated ghost district; AI handles legal logistics, humans handle forbidden cargo.
- The Forest: Wooder territory; anti-corp, handcrafted wooden deck doctrine.
- The Roads: corridor battleground, not a normal district, but treated as district-equivalent for operations.
- Electropolis: canonically real but withheld; security-showcase corridor under the Fuzz, future reveal zone.

Protagonist Pressure Spine (Skids)

Skip “Skids” Mayhew starts as a Batteryville gutter courier (Punch Skater rung), with personal gravity tied to The Grid through his disappeared parents.

His orbit tightens as factions converge:
- Moonrisers notice him in Nightshade.
- Asclepians use his clean profile as transport cover.
- Mesopotamian Society and Knights Technarchy pull him into the hidden artifact corridor.
- Qu111s intend to weaponize the eventual fallout as city-scale truth release.

This is the current plot machine: labor exploitation, medical necessity, truth warfare, relic obsession, and cult pressure colliding around one rider who was supposed to stay small.
