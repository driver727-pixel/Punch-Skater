# Factions

The City's courier underground is organized into **crews** — autonomous collectives
that share territory, training, resources, and reputation. A courier's crew is their
family, their employer, and their legal protection (such as it is).

There are also the corporations and their affiliated brands, which shape the equipment
market and the danger landscape every courier operates in.

---

## Crews

### Nightshade Runners
*District: Nightshade — "The original crew. Everything started here."*

The oldest active courier crew in the City. The Nightshade Runners have been running
packages through the neon alleys since before the underground had a name. They are
the cultural center of the courier network — other crews measure themselves against
the Runners' standards, and disputes between factions are often brought to a Runners
elder for arbitration.

They run an open-recruitment policy in Nightshade proper: anyone who can complete a
trial run without getting flagged gets a week's probation. The washout rate is 60%.
The ones who survive are lifers.

### Chrome Blades
*Districts: Airaway, Glass City — "Precision is the only luxury that matters."*

Corporate-adjacent and proud of it — but not corp-owned. Chrome Blades are elite
couriers who run high-value intelligence packages for clients who can pay for
discretion at the executive level. They look the part: gear maintained to showroom
standard, routes planned with operational precision, no visible underground markings.

Chrome Blades don't see themselves as anti-corp. They see themselves as the
market correcting for HexChain Logistics' incompetence. They'll carry anything for
anyone if the scratch is right and the risk profile is acceptable.

### Neon Ghosts
*Districts: Nightshade, Glass City — "We were never there."*

Specialists in invisible operations. Neon Ghosts are the couriers other couriers
hire when a package absolutely cannot be traced — no biometric flags, no camera
sightings, no delivery confirmation. They operate on a strict anonymity policy: crew
members use code-names only, and client identity is never recorded.

Their reputation is their only public-facing asset, and they protect it fiercely.
A run that goes wrong and gets attributed to a Ghost is career-ending for the
operative involved. The crew's discipline is legendary.

### The Static Pack
*District: The Grid — "Data is the only package that matters. Everything else is just
carrying data in a box."*

Hacker-couriers who treat every run as an intelligence operation. The Static Pack
maintain their own encrypted communications infrastructure — the only crew comms
that Cascade Technologies has never successfully compromised. They double as
information brokers: if you need to know something about a corp's internal operations,
the Pack probably already has it.

Their packages are almost exclusively data chips and physical documents. They refuse
biological and hardware contraband on principle (or so they say — some members are
more principled about this than others).

### Iron Circuit
*District: Batteryville — "We built the infrastructure. We know every crack in it."*

Military-trained courier collective with deep roots in Batteryville's plant-worker
unions. Iron Circuit was founded by ex-Axiom soldiers who took the skills they'd built
protecting corporate assets and redirected them toward protecting the people those
assets exploited.

They run the most organized operation of any crew — formal shifts, standardized
training, equipment maintained in a shared depot. They are also the most politically
active: Iron Circuit has a standing policy of running union intelligence and
organizing materials at reduced rates.

### The Undercurrent
*District: Nightshade (Undercity) — "The surface is for people who haven't found
a better way yet."*

Operators of the Undercity — the sub-basement tunnel network beneath Nightshade.
The Undercurrent knows routes through the City's maintenance infrastructure that
appear on no official map. They move packages that can't risk being spotted at street
level: high-profile fugitives, mass contraband loads, documents that would end careers
or start wars.

Joining the Undercurrent requires a personal introduction from an existing member.
They don't advertise, don't recruit publicly, and don't explain themselves to anyone.

### Voltage Saints
*District: Batteryville — "Speed is sacred. Hesitation is sin."*

A semi-religious courier collective that treats exceptional speed as a form of
spiritual practice. Voltage Saints conduct pre-run rituals — equipment checks
performed in a specific sequence, route-planning meditations, group affirmations that
sound like liturgy to outsiders. It works: their average delivery time across
Batteryville routes is consistently 15% faster than any competing crew.

Their theology is loose: the general doctrine holds that the City's energy flows
through conduit and courier alike, and those who move fastest are most aligned with
that flow. New members are called Initiates. The fastest runner in the crew at any
given time holds the title of Saint.

### The Dark Lanes
*District: Nightshade — "There are back-routes and there are back-routes."*

Specialists in off-grid transit. The Dark Lanes maintain a network of illegal
passage routes through the City — bricked-up service tunnels cleared and mapped,
private property crossings negotiated through bribery or force, rooftop paths that
technically don't exist. They license these routes to other crews for a fee, and
run their own packages exclusively through them.

The Dark Lanes have a reputation for operating in the legal grey zone that makes even
other underground crews slightly nervous. They're not corp informants — the corps hate
them as much as anyone — but their business relationships are complicated.

### Circuit Breakers
*District: Batteryville — "We deliver your package and break something on the way.
Two services, one price."*

Anti-corporate saboteurs who fund their activism through courier work. Every Circuit
Breakers run includes, at minimum, one minor infrastructure disruption: a corp
surveillance node taken offline, a HexChain tracking beacon destroyed, a power
junction tapped for the underground's benefit.

Other crews find them disruptive (hence the name) and occasionally irresponsible, but
no one questions their commitment. When Axiom runs a major enforcement sweep, Circuit
Breakers are always on the front line of the counter-response.

### Phantom Riders
*Districts: Airaway, The Grid — "Who runs the Phantoms? Nobody knows. That's the
point."*

The most mysterious crew in the network. No one knows the Phantom Riders' membership.
No one can reliably contact them — jobs are left at specific drop-points and either
get picked up or don't. No crew markings, no communal space, no public-facing
leadership.

What is known: the Phantoms are extremely active in the City's most surveilled
districts, they specialize in the highest-risk packages, and their fee structure is
reportedly tied to the probability that they'll be caught. So far, no confirmed
Phantom Rider has ever been detained by corp security.

## Manufacturers

Equipment brands shape a courier's capabilities and signal their aesthetic to anyone
who knows how to read the gear.

### VoltEdge
*"Faster by design."*
High-output electric propulsion boards and wheel systems. VoltEdge is the top-shelf
choice for speed-focused couriers — expensive, high-maintenance, and worth every
credit. Their signature feature is the adaptive torque system that automatically
adjusts to terrain resistance.

### NightRider Tech
*"Built for places that don't want to be found."*
Stealth-optimized equipment — low acoustic signature wheels, signal-dampened chassis,
matte-finish surfaces that don't catch surveillance camera light. NightRider Tech is
the default brand for Neon Ghosts and Ninjas who care about remaining invisible.

### ChromeCraft
*"Because your gear says everything before you do."*
Premium alloy construction with a signature mirror-chrome aesthetic. ChromeCraft
boards are the status symbol of choice in Airaway and Glass City. The performance is
real, but so is the price tag.

### NeonForge
*"Customizable from the ground up."*
Mid-market boards with an emphasis on customization and style. NeonForge sells
modular systems that couriers can reconfigure for different run types. Popular in
Nightshade for the extensive third-party modification ecosystem.

### StaticWave
*"When the grid goes down, we keep rolling."*
EMP-hardened equipment for The Grid and environments with active electronic
countermeasures. StaticWave boards are less flashy than VoltEdge but will keep
functioning after a Cascade Technologies countermeasure sweep.

### IronPulse
*"Military spec, street price."*
Rugged, high-durability equipment originally designed for Axiom Dynamics security
forces. IronPulse boards found their way into the underground when ex-military
couriers brought their issued gear with them. Heavy but nearly indestructible.

### ShadowDrive
*"Unregistered. Untracked. Yours."*
Black-market hardware with no manufacturer's registry, no embedded tracking chips,
and no warranty. ShadowDrive exists in a legal no-man's-land that makes it
indispensable to couriers who need to move without leaving a hardware signature.

### ApexRoll
*"Competition-grade. No compromises."*
The preferred brand of ex-Olympic athletes and sponsored couriers. ApexRoll boards
are engineered to competition specifications and priced accordingly. Their customer
base is narrow but loyal.

### CyberGlide
*"Smarter than the route."*
AI-assisted navigation boards that build and update routing models in real time.
CyberGlide's onboard systems map obstacles, security positions, and optimal lines
automatically. Hackers love them; everyone else finds the AI a bit opinionated.

### VoidRacer
*"No registry. No history. No problem."*
The most stripped-down brand on the market — pure propulsion, no electronics, no
digital footprint of any kind. VoidRacer boards cannot be remotely disabled, cannot
be pinged, and cannot be ID-matched to their rider. The corps would ban them if they
could find the factory.
